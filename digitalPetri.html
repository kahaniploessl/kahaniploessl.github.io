digitalPetri

Demo Video: Watch Here!


digitalPetri is a multiplayer, generative video game installation comprised of a projected screen and several scattered controllers. Viewers are invited to pick up a controller and imbue themselves into the piece as ever evolving digital avatārs. Players are able to shape shift into new glitched beings, dynamically interact with other avatārs, and cover the scene with the trails of their inimitable history, only to be painted over by whoever comes next.

As a digital artifact, digitalPetri’s avatārs relinquish their structural code to the players through real-time mesh deformations. Players do not simply simulate change—they become active agents in the formation of the work. By interacting with the piece, they rewrite the very data of their avatār’s digital makeup, twisting thousands of vertices into infinitely unique structures. Furthermore, this distortion is made unbounded. Avatārs may shrink into the size of a couple of pixels or eclipse beyond the edges of the screen—what happens to their form is up to the player, serving a reflection of their intent. Glitches are not an error, but a welcomed result of the piece.

Evoking a petri dish of glitches, digitalPetri depicts the transverse of the digital as a process of endless iteration attempting to understand change. digitalPetri challenges this idea of digital memory and the fallacy that digitizing inherently preserves an empirical understanding of these states. As soon as a glitched being begins to proliferate, so too does it overwrite. Each new action spawns a new layer of growth and decay—avatars shift, disappear, and reform in continuous cycles. In digitalPetri, preservation is not the goal; instead, it embraces transformation, impermanence, and the beauty of what cannot be saved. pixelWater is an interactive webpage exploring the digital immortalization of fleeting moments and the fluidity of internet spaces.
